Forms and the Web

On the importance of forms or what are forms good for:
Forms are ubiquitous across the depth and breadth of the internet. Nearly all of e-commerce makes use of forms in some or all of their sites. While we see and use these forms every day it's easy to forget their uses and potential pitfalls. Forms are at their heart simple, input some data to a form upload that data to somewhere in the cloud. However there is several topics that should be further investigated. Accessibility: In much the same way as the previous paper, accessibility is very important, using various means at our disposal we can both build both good looking forms for our customers as well as ensuring all of our potential customers can access our sites with ease. Examples of these include the title attribute and user notifications to ensure clear communication with the users. Semantic content: In a similar manner to accessibility, using the correct tags can allow for a better use experience while also creating a good semantic flow which will both allow search engine crawlers to better categorize our content as well as and creating a nice structure to our page. Ease of use: In much the same way as the previous two topics - its very important to create a good user experience. An excellent and relevant example is the WA state unemployment website, every week hundreds of thousands of people log onto the website to fill out their weekly claim ensuring that they have a simple and easy time filling out the required information. Using industry best practices including clearly labeling your forms, not using any more forms than necessary - or removing unnecessary or superfluous inputs.
